Fellow Facepunch/Minecraft folks, I present to you a vanilla redstone conundrum. So I built this somewhat long underground minecart system to transport myself safely between three destinations (let's just call them A, B, and C). At one point in the system, the tracks all meet at a three-way intersection. I built a control room at destination A equipped with two redstone lamps to indicate where the junction was set to lead the mine cart, and I planned to do this at the other two destinations as well. I also planned to allow the junction to be altered at any one of the destinations, to facilitate travel from each. Now, silly redstone-impaired me thought that this would be simple. I thought I could simply flip a redstone torch on and off to get the track to orient in the correct directions. Unfortunately, I forgot about the south-east rule, and I also forgot that I have no idea how to use redstone circuits. It turns out logic gates are much simpler in theory than in a game world with physical limitations (Wiremod was way easier than this). After a few minutes trying to figure out this mystical south-east phenomenon, I ended up building the intersection as a ring, with a single empty block in the middle. One redstone torch was placed under each turn, making three in total - the correct combination would have to be set to reach the desired destination. The redstone lamps in the control rooms would indicate when the correct setup was made via buttons. [I]In theory[/I], this design should work. I know that I need to use some sort of latch system to allow for buttons from all three control rooms to correctly toggle the redstone torches regardless of source. I also need some kind of two-way repeater system that both sends signal to the torches and reads the output of them for the lamps. In the case that I added a fourth destination, I would also need to know how to negate input to the lamps because this fourth place would have a turn in the middle of another already-made rail. However, I simply have no idea how to put it all together - how do I make the needed circuitry? I made a crude MS Paint drawing to attempt to illustrate this predicament.
