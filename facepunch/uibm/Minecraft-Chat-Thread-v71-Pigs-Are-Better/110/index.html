If you hover over an item and press "r", you'll see how to craft said item. With a few exceptions, this'll show if you need a specific machine to get it. If you press "u" over the item, it'll show you all the recipes that that item is used in. If it's not turning up any recipes, that's when you should look up the mod it's from, or if you're not sure about that, just google the name of the item and "minecraft mod" and it'll usually give you a wiki page on it. If you're at a crafting bench and you look at a recipe, it'll have a little ? to the bottom right of the recipe. Clicking on that will add ghost blocks to the crafting table to make crafting easier. A handy tip: If you shift+click it, it'll try to autocomplete the recipe with the items in your inventory, and if you're missing anything you need to craft it it won't.

There's a mod called What Am I Looking At that adds what mod an item is from. You can also type @Mod.modname to filter out to items from that mod. Though if you do have WAILA you can just type the mod name and it'll generally do the same thing since it adds it to the tooltip. But that's not its primary purpose, which is (shockingly enough) to show you the name of what entities, blocks, or liquids you are looking at, sometimes with additional information depending on the mod (You can see the amount of power in a thermal expansion machine for example)
Another useful NEI tip: If you click on the top of the NEI menu, where it says "Item Subsets", you can then go to "Mod" and shift click one of the mods to show all the blocks and items listed in that mod.
