[img]http://img196.imageshack.us/img196/140/jfzu.png[/img] Ethanol production line using nothing but a Steve's Carts oak tree farm, Natura berries and MFR Harvesters/Sludge Boiler. There's 3 "bush bricks" of each berry type that can be squeezed into fruit juice, each measuring 7x15x6 (currently 6 high, planning on expanding to 10 high) with an equal height arrangement of MFR Harvesters with gold range upgrades (had a shitload of gold lying around so figured "why not?"). Berries are deposited into AE system which takes them to 2 Squeezers, with a 3rd for processing apples only from the tree farm (also for mulch). The fruit juice is then used to ferment saplings from the tree farm (using mulch instead of fertilizers, more renewable), then further processed in an array of 4 stills. Currently using ethanol to fuel a max-sized high-pressure boiler. When it started, I had about 800 buckets worth of ethanol stored up from a previous smaller setup that used a low-pressure boiler and some magmatic engines to power the setup (MUCH smaller bush and manual sapling/mulch feed), the HP boiler sucked it down to about 200 buckets worth, and as of this post is has gone back up to roughly 800 buckets worth being stored in a 5x5x7 Railcraft tank. The plan is to have at least 4 HP boilers running Industrial engines and an additional 2 powering a handful of steam turbines. While so far it's proving to be a success, I can't say I'd recommend it for everyone, as acquiring that many damned bushes takes a good deal of time and/or bonemeal. Thankfully there's the Watering Can. :v:

[QUOTE=Zero-Point;42839456]Couldn't hurt to try.[/QUOTE] Found the solution. I'm a dummy and need to learn to read the books in the game. According to his book GregTech Manual I (4e), a single bronze blast furnace requires a hundred bronze [b]plates[/b] (or [i]two hundred bronze bars[/i], and the steam forge hammer still requires two bars per plate!) Early steel does not require coal dust, but coal itself. Where the coal dust goes in the industrial blast furnace, you put something burnable, like coal or coal coke. It uses two coal coke or four coal per ingot. Blocks of said coals will not work. Protip: If a mod author provides a written book involving their mod, read it because it may have updated.
