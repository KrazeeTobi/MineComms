Any tips on building with stone, cobble, stone bricks etc. I pretty much exclusively build with wood and can never get a stone brick house to look nice.
[QUOTE=itsthejayden;44221503]Any tips on building with stone, cobble, stone bricks etc. I pretty much exclusively build with wood and can never get a stone brick house to look nice.[/QUOTE] Well, playing in Beta 1.7.3 gives you a lot of experience with building stone structures without using stone brick. Might want to try it sometime. One of the most important things to remember is that all types of stone are shades of gray, which is the most difficult part to work with. To start, I've found that cobblestone is best used for foundation work. For example, I'm currently building a council building on top of a sizeable hill, so I added a huge amount dirt to make a flat building area. The massive cube of dirt is pretty hideous, though, so I outlined it in cobble. However, it's a necessity to add alcoves to visually break up the texturing, or it will look like a giant box. Regular stone is also important, and I generally use it for the basic structure of a building. You don't [I]always[/I] need to make the design too complex, though - stone has a pleasing look, and a lot of detail can be distracting to the eye. However, adding one-block recesses to hold torches is a good way to add light without spamming torches across the walls. Sculpting arches and vaults is a good idea for underground structures, too. Surprisingly enough, glass is almost a necessity in my designs. Look at real-life windows - most of them are sunken into the surface of a building's walls, so do the same in Minecraft. Just be sure to add plenty of torches for when the sun goes down. Finally, stone slabs are useful for broad details, like creating a facade or walkways.
