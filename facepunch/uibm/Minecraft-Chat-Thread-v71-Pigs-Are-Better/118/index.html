The new enchanting is actually pretty decent when you understand all the changes: [B]*[/B] xp now has an overall lower amount (10 levels before is now about 3 levels) [B]*[/B] you get a preview if at least 1 enchantment you will be getting [B]*[/B] you can't actually change the previews until you enchant something (if max for a diamond sword is shitty, you have to enchant something else to make the diamond sword enchant change) [B]*[/B] you don't actually spend the 30/16/6 levels to get each of the enchantment levels, instead you spend only 1-3 depending on which level of enchantment you pick (top is 1, bottom is 3) [B]*[/B] the levels are required to unlock that enchantment level (30 for max, but you only spend 3 levels to acquire the enchantment) [B]*[/B] lapis is used as a sort-of balancing item so you have to actually go mining deep and find it before you can even have a chance at enchanting. [B]*[/B] bookshelves are still required like normal [B]*[/B] because of the higher amount of xp required per level, you can either just bruteforce your level and get the highest every time, or you can strategically enchant books for combination [B]*[/B] also because you don't actually spend the levels needed to unlock the enchant, you can enchant more things once you finally reach level 30 (it takes at least 30 levels to get the max enchant at first, but then subsequent max enchants only take 3 levels), this makes it even more of a penalty to die and lose all your levels Overall I think it's a good change that probably still needs a few tweaks (this is still just the first preview of the new enchanting in the first snapshot of the year)
