[QUOTE=AWarGuy;42388944]I can't get Rei's minimap mod working at all. I've followed the guides where you copy versions, rename , install mods etc and then picked the minimap versions I've made etc and it doesn't work. I've followed several guides on youtube and nothing works at all, I need a minimap mod or any mod similar to it otherwise I get lost incredibly quickly.[/QUOTE] Consider these alternatives: [url=http://www.minecraftforum.net/topic/1116564-164-162-mamiyaotarus-mods-voxelmap-chatbubbles-etc/]VoxelMap[/url]- This is the one included with FTB so I guess in a way it could be considered "standard." Very easy to use, especially if you're familiar with Rei's. [url=http://www.minecraftforum.net/topic/1570989-162-forge-mapwriter-an-open-source-mini-map/]MapWriter[/url]- I personally use this one. It blends power and ease-of-use to deliver a minimap mod that does things that few others have, such as waypoint categorization and saving & exporting of the entire map without being too "cheaty;" this is the only one of the bunch that doesn't have a mob radar or an overpowered cave mode. [url=http://www.minecraftam.com/]Minecraft AutoMap[/url]- This is the most powerful minimap mod I know of, but it requires an external application to use and tends to eat up system resources. It's also the most "cheaty," with features such as warning sounds when creepers get to close and the ability to peer through stone to find minerals, and more. As a last resort, consider investing in a notebook or using a text file and writing down the F3 coordinates in that. [editline]3rd October 2013[/editline] Actually now that I come to think of it, try spending a bit of time with manually writing coordinates down. It might force you to practice studying your surroundings to make a memory path to your home base. This is something that I like to practice on top of using my minimaps.
