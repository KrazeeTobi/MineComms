Long, flowing golden hair, brilliant blue eyes, it almost pained me to see her go. You see, she was an explorationist, as I am. She didn't want to take part in my profession, but I kept pushing her to join me on an expedition, and she eventually gave in, and she loved it. Of course it would happen that she didn't die that way. She came down with a nasty case of consumption, which some might know as tuberculosis. We were unable to treat her, as we were in the Mayan jungle searching for the lost treasure of Na'Aptin at the time. As it was, I have not dated since. I've found that a lonely lifestyle is easy to get used to when you're accompanied by so many things at once that could kill you. But here, it is always so peaceful, like a slice of heaven given to a young child, eager to explore the boundless nature of god's creation. Of course, I have not yet seen such a god, but if he exists, he may make it known to me. Some more cave has been dug out yesterday. I tunneled around the gate as I'd planned to do, but inside the chest was some sort of book in some lost language. I couldn't read it, but from a reoccurring word followed by the scribbling, the language was apparently called 'Java'. Some sort of derivative of the ancient Indian language 'Janya', I assume. I will spend my nights trying to decode the language, as I have not been able to sleep regularly recently. Curious as it is, the birds are louder when I stay in the cave at night. I tried going back to my base camp but it was infested with sheep. Dozens of them had flocked around the lights. Light may be more important on this island than I'd originally thought. As always. -J.D.
