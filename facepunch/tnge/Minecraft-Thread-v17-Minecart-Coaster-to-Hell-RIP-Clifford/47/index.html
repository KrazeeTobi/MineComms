I've seen some suggestions for multiplayer on the fourms that seem to make it into nothing more but simplified Wurm. Stuff like totems, town blocks, and the like. I really hope Notch implements none of these, and for good reason. I propose a less restrictive, but just as protective, system: a trust list. If you're not on someone's list, blocks constructed by them take 5 times as long as normal (and if the total is over 10 seconds, you can't destroy that block at all, except with TNT or something along those lines). People could add other players to their trust list if they wanted building partners, or a town could add each other for help. This would stop people from clawing their way through an iron cistern to flood a town, but it would still allow large-scale, non-griefy invasions (like wars and such). If someone still griefs with this system, so what? There is a multitude of ways to deal with them, the most obvious being to kill them. You could also get your buddies to knock them out and put them in a makeshift jail, expel them and tell others to do the same, maybe even forgive them (!)... the list goes on and on. These only exist [i]if the restrictions on the player are as small as possible![/i] Besides, how many griefers will there be? The game will cost ~$24 dollars at launch, correct? Unless multiple character slots are implemented, you're gonna be hard-pressed to find someone who'd pay $24 dollars to get shot up and stabbed for the rest of his Minecraft life. Sure, they'll be SOME griefers, but not too many it's overpowering. I will be severly disappointed if this turns into another Wurm, because the freedom possible here is staggering.
