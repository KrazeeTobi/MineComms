[B]PART FIVE: HEALTH AND WHAT YOU CAN DO TO GET IT BACK[/B] [img]http://www.minecraftwiki.net/images/d/d9/Heart.png[/img][img]http://www.minecraftwiki.net/images/d/d9/Heart.png[/img][img]http://www.minecraftwiki.net/images/d/d9/Heart.png[/img][img]http://www.minecraftwiki.net/images/d/d9/Heart.png[/img][img]http://www.minecraftwiki.net/images/d/d9/Heart.png[/img][img]http://www.minecraftwiki.net/images/d/d9/Heart.png[/img][img]http://www.minecraftwiki.net/images/d/d9/Heart.png[/img][img]http://www.minecraftwiki.net/images/d/d9/Heart.png[/img][img]http://www.minecraftwiki.net/images/d/d9/Heart.png[/img][img]http://www.minecraftwiki.net/images/7/7a/Half_Heart.png[/img] This is a series of hearts. You have ten of these on your health bar, just near the inventory rectangle thing that doesn't have an official name. They can be fully removed by a hit, or half-removed by drowning, falling, and explosions, giving you a total of 20 hitpoints before you die if you want to get specific. When your health begins to get dangerously low, your health bar will shake up and down to get your attention. [B]HOW YOU LOSE HEALTH[/B] There are basically five ways to lose health. Four have existed since survival, and one was added recently as part of a bug/glitch patch. Falling: You can fall from two block's worth of height without taking any damage. Anything above that, including your jumping height, will cause you to take 1/2 a heart per block. Certain grounds, such as the bedrock at the bottom of a floating map, will kill you instantly no matter the height if you are high enough to take fall damage. In order to prevent falling damage, you can use flint (see crafting guide) to set yourself on fire, as you will not take any fall damage while something else is hurting you. Suffocating, Drowning, and Burning: All of these do a 1/2 heart of damage per second. When underwater, you have a breath gauge just above your health bar. If all of the bubbles pop, you will begin losing health. Coming up for air will instantly give you your breath back, but not your health. Burning and Suffocating do constant damage. If you are on fire, a water source of 2 blocks or higher will extinguish you. If you happen to fall in lava, it does between 3 and 4 hearts of damage per second you are in it, and in addition to the burning damage, it can be lethal. To suffocate, there must be a block on your head, blocking your vision. It's hard to pull off by normal means, but it can happen, and the only way out is to break the block on top of you. Monster Damage: Monsters do varying amounts of damage. Way back in the enemy part of the guide, I went into minor detail about it. Some do 1 or 2 with melee, and things of that nature. A creeper can kill you in one hit if it is directly next to you when it explodes. [B]HOW TO REGAIN HEALTH[/B] So, you're down to your last health bar. Good job, must have taken some effort! Kidding, it's very easy to lose health in this game, from careless falls to accidental enemy touching. No biggie, here's what you can do: [B]1. Mushrooms[/B] Heals for: [img]http://www.minecraftwiki.net/images/d/d9/Heart.png[/img][img]http://www.minecraftwiki.net/images/d/d9/Heart.png[/img][img]http://www.minecraftwiki.net/images/d/d9/Heart.png[/img][img]http://www.minecraftwiki.net/images/d/d9/Heart.png[/img][img]http://www.minecraftwiki.net/images/d/d9/Heart.png[/img] In survival, brown mushrooms heal you, while red mushrooms hurt you. They must be combined with a bowl to use them in Indev. (see crafting guide) First, gather a mushroom of each type ([img]http://www.echidnatribe.org/Minecraft/img/blocks/red_mushroom.png[/img] and [img]http://www.echidnatribe.org/Minecraft/img/blocks/brown_mushroom.png[/img]). Then, craft them vertically with a bowl ([img]http://www.echidnatribe.org/Minecraft/img/items/bowl.png[/img]). The resulting mushroom soup should look like this: [img]http://www.echidnatribe.org/Minecraft/img/items/soup.png[/img]. You can then put it in your inventory and right click to eat it. Mushroom soup restores 5 hearts, more than any other food, but it requires more effort to make, as both mushroom types are rare. [B]2. Pork and Grilled Pork[/B] Heals for: [img]http://www.minecraftwiki.net/images/d/d9/Heart.png[/img][img]http://www.minecraftwiki.net/images/7/7a/Half_Heart.png[/img] or [img]http://www.minecraftwiki.net/images/d/d9/Heart.png[/img][img]http://www.minecraftwiki.net/images/d/d9/Heart.png[/img][img]http://www.minecraftwiki.net/images/d/d9/Heart.png[/img][img]http://www.minecraftwiki.net/images/d/d9/Heart.png[/img] Pork is the easiest healing item to get. All you have to do is kill a pig, which may seem morally wrong, but the other pigs don't seem to care all that much if you take one of their buddies. Killing a pig will produce 0-2 pork ([img]http://www.echidnatribe.org/Minecraft/img/items/meat.png[/img]), more often 2 than not. Eating pork raw will give you back a meager 1.5 hearts. Pork, annoyingly, does not stack, meaning you can fill whole chests with just pork, only to waste them all in one go. That is, unless you put them in a furnace and smelt them. The produced grilled pork ([img]http://www.echidnatribe.org/Minecraft/img/items/cooked_meat.png[/img]) heals four whole hearts and is the second highest healing item. [B]3. Bread[/B] Heals for: [img]http://www.minecraftwiki.net/images/d/d9/Heart.png[/img][img]http://www.minecraftwiki.net/images/d/d9/Heart.png[/img][img]http://www.minecraftwiki.net/images/7/7a/Half_Heart.png[/img] Bread ([img]http://www.echidnatribe.org/Minecraft/img/items/bread.png[/img]) requires a little effort to make (see the next part for a farming ), but can produce mass payoff. Here is what you need to create bread: -2 Planks -2 Durability items (Wood/gold/iron/diamond/stone) -A Water Source (Optional) -About 9-20 Grass tiles, depending how easily you obtain seeds -A Sunny (and sheep/pig/creeper free) area to grow your crops in -A Workbench Once you complete the farming (again, covered in the next part), you'll have wheat ([img]http://www.echidnatribe.org/Minecraft/img/items/wheat.png[/img]), which is stackable unlike pork. Craft three wheat in a vertical row, and voila, you have bread. [B]4. As a last resort[/B] If you switch to Peaceful mode, your hearts will automatically regenerate, and all monsters will be cleared from the game. Some consider it the wuss way of doing things, but it's nice when you've got no other choice. [B]HOW TO KEEP YOUR HEARTS SAFE[/B] The only way you can take hits without any harm coming to you is with armor. [img]http://www.minecraftwiki.net/images/5/5a/Armorc.png[/img] These are all of the armors you can have currently. From left to right, they are gold, diamond, iron, and cloth. In order of strength, they are as follows: Cloth - Like another healthbar. Can barely take 20 hits before breaking. Gold - Basically cloth armor but shinier. Iron - Can take 1/2 a health bar's worth more hits than Cloth armor. Diamond - Worth two health bars in armor. Armor prevents all damage to you while it still has durability, and I mean all damage. You cannot drown, suffocate, or burn while you have armor. As well, your hearts are perfectly preserved in their current state until the armor runs out. If you have one heart left, it is a legitimate tactic to make some armor for artificial health before going out for food. There are four types of armor: Helmets, Chestplates, Leggings, and Boots. There is no difference between them and they will support exactly the same amount. Wearing more than one armor piece at a time will give you the durability of both items on your armor bar, which appears near the inventory bar thing that doesn't have a name just as your health does.
