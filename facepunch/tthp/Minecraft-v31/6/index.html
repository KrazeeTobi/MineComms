I was going to post this when it was relevant but then FP went down so here it is anyway: [highlight]DOOMISH'S GUIDE ON HOW TO AVOID BEING CALLED A BEGGAR![/highlight] [b]SO, YOU WANT TO RECEIVE MINECRAFT?[/b] First off, you must consider your reason. Just saying "buy me Minecraft" will not get you the game. You must put conscious effort into your reasoning. For example: "I don't have the money!" is not a good excuse. "Paypal won't accept my card/I can't use Paypal in my country!" is a good excuse. [b]HAVE A GOOD REASON? LET'S MOVE ON. HERE IS HOW YOU SHOULD ASK.[/b] There are a few ways to ask for someone to buy you Minecraft. It should always include a way of payment if you can't buy it yourself. Here are two ways: 1. Buy them something on Steam Minecraft is only 15 dollars max, and there are tons of games on Steam for around that price. Ask if there is something someone would be willing to trade for it. See if there is a way to negotiate a good price to settle on. 2. Just give them the money Of course, this option may not be applicable if you can't use Paypal in your country, but it's still an option nonetheless. If you are desperate and have no money onhand, you could always ask with this as your reasoning, but I don't recommend it. 3. Write Minecraft on your penis No, I'm not joking. It worked once in the past. [b]SO, HOW DO YOU GO ABOUT POSTING, YOU MAY ASK?[/b] Here are two examples of posts, not made by actual people in the thread, but representative of the different types of mindests. [quote=Beggar]Please buy me Minecraft, I don't have any money! It's too hot out to mow some lawns! I really want to play Survival MP with you guys![/quote] [quote=Asker]I'd be willing to trade 15 dollars worth of Steam games for Minecraft, please PM me if you're interested! I really want to play Survival MP with you guys![/quote] You may have noticed the subtle difference in tone between the Beggar and the Asker. The Beggar does not offer anything in return, and wants instant gratification. Yes, he asked nicely, but it takes more than kind words to get someone to spend their valuable money on you. The Asker, on the other hand, demonstrates the previous two steps, and offers something in return without being rude about it. Neither of them were rude, in fact, quite the opposite, but there is a fine line between what people will call asking and what people will call begging, so be cautious. [b]SO, YOU HAVE ASKED FOR SOMEONE TO BUY YOU MINECRAFT. NOW WHAT?[/b] Now comes the hard part: Only doing it once. Asking more than once or twice at the most can be fatal to your reputation. Even if you offer something in return, some will still insist you are begging if you ask more than a few times. "Repost for new page" is not a reliable tactic and will get the same treatment. If you wait it out a bit, you may get a good response, and if you are lucky, you'll have Minecraft in no time! Of course, this all only applies if you have a legitimate reason for wanting someone to buy you the game, but if you follow these tips, you will stay out of the red zone and off of the shunning list! :eng101:
