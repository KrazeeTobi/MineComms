Notch already mentioned that. As for the rest... remember that Minecraft is primarily a building game. And indev is primarily a survival game. So the main focus isn't farming; farming is merely a means to and end (in this case, getting food to heal yourself). Blueprints would destroy the main purpose of Minecraft - which is building everything one block at a time subject to your resources. Notch has stated several times that he wants Minecraft to be similar to dwarf fortress in survival mode. So farming will be just one aspect of the game that will make survival easier. Notch has also stated that he wants things to stay pretty simple and DIY. Automated tasks or a complex farming system wouldn't really fit with that.
