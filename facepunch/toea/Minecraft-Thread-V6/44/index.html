I absolutely love this map the map generator gave me. it started out as a simple project to test the efficiency of digging a mine straight into the ground rather than surface mining, but turned into the creation of a huge lava fueled base. It taught me some things about lava too, which I will share with you at the end of these pictures. (Note: These were taken after the initial set up of the base, everything was dug and placed by hand, no map editor was used) As you can see, the game placed me in a very bad starting position, fortunately the water broke my fall. [img]http://filesmelt.com/dl/1notch1.png[/img] I had grabbed some stuff when I started, but I wanted to get back for some none craftable items. Using the massive amounts of stone I have, stairs were no problem. [img]http://filesmelt.com/dl/1notch2.png[/img] I tried to hide the entrance to the initial mine so it was harder for creepers to accidently stumble into and also I wanted to test out disguised entrances. The disguise worked pretty well, the entrace is the opening under my crosshairs [img]http://filesmelt.com/dl/1notch3.png[/img] The bottom of the stairs where I had stumbled upon two large magma pools. Using techniques I had thought up earlier, I dug a system of Lava fueled lighting for my mine. [img]http://filesmelt.com/dl/1notch5.png[/img] One of the hallways with lava lighting. At the end of the hallway and to the right is where I found... [img]http://filesmelt.com/dl/1notch6.png[/img] This massive cavern. Orriginally there were small empty pools on the floor, however I built a magma pipe system and used it to fill the pools, which lit up the cavern. [img]http://filesmelt.com/dl/1notch7.png[/img] This is my favorite spot, it's right behind the huge rock column at the far side of the cavern. I think it's neat the map generator actually created the column like that. [img]http://filesmelt.com/dl/1notch8.png[/img] A channel I had dug to light up more of the cavern, there's way more to the cavern at the other side of the channel, which is why I stopped the flow so I could explore it before flooding it with lava. [img]http://filesmelt.com/dl/1notch9.png[/img] That's a hollow passage that I explored and nearly got lost in. The cavern is HUGE and there are a few pools of water and more magma back there as well. [img]http://filesmelt.com/dl/1notch10.png[/img] A few things I learned about magma is that: 1.Magma is a GREAT tool for lighting 2.However as you drain it, you're going to eventually run out. I did and had to use magma spawn blocks to keep the supply going. 3.For reasons like this, magma could become a commodity in survival mode, there's only a limited amount and it has many uses. 4.If magma supplies get low and the lava can't fill the area it's on, it will start solidifying. Meaning that you have to go back and remove the solidified magma chunks from your magma pipes. Which is a pain. 5.Because of that, I would plan on creating primary magma pipes 2 high. It makes it easier to clean and lowers the chance of total clogging.
