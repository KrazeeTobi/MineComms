I can get as far as 1k blocks before dying horribly.[/QUOTE] If you're new to 2b2t, don't plan on surviving the first time. It will take to many tries to survive outside of spawn. Getting out of spawn itself isn't too difficult. It's about not starving before you can find food. Un-Escapable Spawn - Sometimes spawn will be trapped by spawn fags, keeping it barricaded with lava/cobblestone/obsidian. Or they'll be slaughtering everything that breathes. In this case you'll have to log in at a different time and try again. (You won't make it otherwise.) Starvation - This is the leading cause of death to newcomers on the server. Never run and/or jump unless it's completely necessary. (Escaping death/jumping to get past an obstacle.) Don't stay to fight aggressive mobs, you're wasting your time. (Unless it's a zombie, there's a chance it'll drop some rotton flesh.) Your best chances of finding food are discovering hidden stashes. Or having other players give it to you. (Not likely.) Aggressive Mobs - Skeleton Archers especially, those guys suck. Getting Lost in the Nether - Going into the Nether is useless unless you have a guaranteed way out. The Nether landscape around spawn is almost as bad as the overworld's. (Spawn fags will tamper with the portals, leaving no way out.) Other Players - Though this is less likely outside of the 2,000 block area around spawn; most will kill on sight. When traveling it's advised that you don't stick around too long. Lurkers like to set up traps full of food, then ambush you. (Not a common occurance.) Holes in the ground - You have to remember that thousands of players have tread the ground around spawn. If you don't watch your step; you'll plummet to the bedrock before you can save yourself. Escaping Spawn is not a race, don't treat it as one. Just take it at a steady pace and you'll make it. [editline]18th June 2013[/editline] Now that you have made your way out of the spawn, and you have a sufficient amount of food; you'll need to learn how to keep it that way. Finding the Right Place to Base - If you have gotten past the 2k area around spawn you've probably started to notice trees, and possibly passive mobs. I wouldn't advise building above ground because of the close proximity to spawn; but since everyone and their dog has some sort of x-ray mod they'll see you underground too. The decision is ultimately yours. Keeping your Base Hidden - It's inevitable that your base is found if it's built above ground. So don't spend too much time decorating. Work on gathering resources like ores and food etc. Important Resources to Get - Food Finding a Bed - If you cannot find any sheep (High probability you won't.) Then you need to kill spiders and get their silk. Forge some armor and get to looking. You'll need 12 pieces of silk and three wood planks to make a bed. Having a bed is absolutely necessary, because no one likes to accidently to spawn. [editline]18th June 2013[/editline] You peel another layer off of the onion that is 2b2t, just to find a new, completely unrelated problem. You'll grow to realize this is the norm, so get the fuck over it. Nether Portal Temptations - You'll find more and more nether portals outisde the 2,000 block area around spawn, but resist the temptation. You have no need for a nether portal until you have sufficient gear and plenty of food.
