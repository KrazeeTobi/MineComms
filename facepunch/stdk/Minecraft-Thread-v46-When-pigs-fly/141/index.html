I made a little lore behind my land. It all started with three cities. Ejidad (Ee-gee-dad), Seapoint, and Mirus. Ejidad is a enviromental city with many laws, most protecting their coveted lush forests and goergeous wooden houses. The nearby mountain, Holy Hill, is a cave-filled mountain that is extremly difficult to navigate, with pits, monsters, and mines leading down to the center of the earth. Many have gone up and never found again. Ejidad has a large, sprawling cave system beneath it which has never entirely been explored. Seapoint is an extremly defensive city with high walls and almost no way for any monster to enter. It is near a large lake, and has mulitple ports to provide simple transportation around the lake. It is suprisingly a very peaceful city, with farms, mines, docks, houses, and reserch facilities, but the same cannot be said during the night. Monsters have no way of entering the city from the outside, but appear from the darkness inside it. Mirus is a large wooden city resting on a lake with two roads: two wooden plank paths for foot transport, and in between them, a waterway for simple boat transportation. The densly packed city is very prone to fire, so any flint or firestarters are banned from anywhere near the city. Ironicly, the wood is taken from a nearby island with many trees, trees which are cut down, the leaves burned, and the saplings replanted. Marius is primarally a fishing village, and that is definatly their main export. These three cites gradually found each other after a while of exploraiton, and have called themselves the Obsidian Point, whose goal is to learn more about and harness the power of "The Nether", a strange world which is connected to theirs by portals. These portals where randomly found around the land, and were found to be made by creating a frame of obsidian and exposing it to intense heat, which would channel the hot air into a spacial rift which could be used to transport oneself to "The Nether", a strange firey world filled with lava and danger, but being a fast method of travel.
