I was waiting for a new thread to post this, but I'll just put it in the new one too. LONG POST AHEAD. [b]TO CREATE A MINECRAFT RESOURCE MAP USING CARTOGRAPH AND PAINT[/b] 1. Explore as much of your world as you want, as Cartograph only maps areas you've been to. 2. Open Cartograph and create a normal map of your land. Find your civilization or any landmarks for later. 3. Open Cartograph and create a map of the resource you want, by putting its number into the 'Only' box. The block memory numbers are at the end of this post. 4. Paste the map of your resource over your normal land map in MS Paint. Now, it will show you the locations of all of the ore that you selected. [b]Things to remember:[/b] [b]I[/b]f you want to put multiple resources on one map, you would have to color each and every dot from the resource map a colour that isn't already used. It would be easiest to just use multiple maps. [b]O[/b]ne pencil mark in Paint is the same as one block in Minecraft. If you drag the pencil over four Paint pixels, in game you would have to travel four blocks. [b]Y[/b]ou may have to update your maps every once in a while, as when you explore your world then there would be more resource opportunities. [b]T[/b]o find the ore you will probably have to dig for a while, seeing as the map doesn't give distances. A diamond pickaxe is reccomended for digging for your ore. [b]A[/b]n obsidian ore map is difficult to create because each different time you go underground, there is a potential to create more obsidian. [b]T[/b]here's really no use for an air resource map. Derp. [b]S[/b]ome resources, which can only be made through crafting, would be useful to make a map of, as it would also give the location of your civilization if you cannot find it on the world map (As in, make a workbench, make the map, that dot is your civilization).
