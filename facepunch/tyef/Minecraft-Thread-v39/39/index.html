I was thinking about the armor system, and how the armor becomes less and less effective as your armor points approach 0, rather armor being constantly effective up until the point it disappears. It makes me hate wearing weakened armor, but at the same time I don't want to just throw it away. I want to reconstruct it. That's my idea. ARMOR RECONSTRUCTION: Iron, Gold, or Diamond armor can be melted down into their component material in a furnace. The amount of armor points left in the piece of armor determines how much smelt is required to melt it, and how much resource you can reclaim from it. Every smelt will reduce the armor of the piece by 0.5 points (or if the armor is weaker than 0.5, whatever is left) and result in one component resource. So if you were to start reclaiming a sturdy steel chest plate and take it out of the furnace half-way through, you would have 4 bars of steel and a chest plate at 50% durability. Since 24 units of material makes one full 10-point set of armor, reclaiming could at max yield 20 units of resource, making the trade-off unideal at high durability, but favorable at low durability when the armor is closer to useless. This would work the same way for leather armor, except you just throw it on the workbench and the appropriate number of cow hides are output.
