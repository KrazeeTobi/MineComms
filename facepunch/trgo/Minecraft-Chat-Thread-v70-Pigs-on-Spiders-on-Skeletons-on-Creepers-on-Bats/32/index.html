Additionally, at a minimum of 100 heat, snow and ice blocks will melt into water, rendering that block's efficiency to a quarter of what it was. Thankfully, thanks to Xychorium Ice, we don't have to worry about the 100 heat constraint, but the heat freezing from 100 heat to 0 heat. So far, there is no automatic way of melting obsidian back to lava. My plan is a simple single block of ice, two lava source blocks, and a water source block per thermopile. This result has a 200 heat/125 cold. Thanks to thermopile generation running on a minimum function, it will use the smallest number to generate blulectricity. In this case, it will use the 125 cold. Unless going straight to fire which requires a block below it to keep burning, there is no alternative to lava and will need 200 heat, otherwise the min() will use the 100 heat instead of 125 cold. Redundant rambling aside, 0.005*125 (0.625) volts or 0.3*125 (37.5) watts per thermopile. Use the wiki's suggested water-water-water-lava setup if white xychorium is scarce (knowing xychorium, it shouldn't be). This is a perfect, no-maintenance setup for more blulectricity to power your sorting machines, frame quarries, what have you.
