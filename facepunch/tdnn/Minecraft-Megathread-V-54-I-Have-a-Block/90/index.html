I just made the most complicated redstone circuit I've ever made. It is a door locking mechanism, a lever toggles if the door is locked, and if it is unlocked. If the door is locked and the outside pressure pad is pressed, an alarm will go off. There is a button to turn off the alarm inside the house. First what needs to happen is determining whether the door is locked, or unlocked. This is simply done by an AND gate. If the door is unlocked and the pad is pressed, the circuit opens the door. If the door was locked, the door will not open. After determining the locked/unlocked position of the door, it needs to go through another AND gate. This one makes sure that the door is locked and the pad has been stepped on before triggering the alarm. If the door was unlocked, the circuit will not be able to get past this point. If the circuit does make it past there, it goes to a T flip-flop, which toggles the alarm on, and the only way to toggle it off is to press the button inside the house. 2 floors of underground redstone wiring to make sure that a creeper doesn't get into your house.
