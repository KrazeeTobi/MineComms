Reposting my earlier ideas: Brick furnace: An alternative to stone furnaces. It'd would use the coal more effectively, melting/cooking stuff faster, while still burning for the same amount of time. Build with the same pattern as a stone furnace, only with bricks. Camp fire: 1. Fill the 2x2 crafting square with sticks to create timber. 2. Put the timber in the middle of the crafting square (3x3) and then surround it by rock. 3. The camp fire can then be placed and used to provide warmth and light to keep the monsters away. You can also cook stuff on it. - Alternatively, you can use coal instead of the timber pile. - Right clicking with sticks or coal will add coal or timber to the camp fire, extending the burn time. - Can be lighted by right clicking with a torch or by using flint and steel. Grind stone: - Made from 2 refined wood pieces and 7 stone/iron/diamond pieces. - To grind a tool or weapon, you place it in the grindstone and right click the stone continually to make it spin (Each click adds a bit to the progress, how many clicks you need depends on what material you’re grinding.) - It should be faster to grind an item on a diamond grindstone than on a stone grindstone. - A stone grindstone will create a good tool/weapon - A iron grindstone will make a fine tool/weapon - A diamond will make a perfect tool/weapon - If a tool/weapon is good, fine or perfect, it will simply do more damage. Grinding will have no effect on how long a tool/weapon will last. - If you combine the grindstone with a furnace, you can put some coal in the grindstone and it will automatically grind what you’ve put inside it, instead of you constantly clicking the grindstone. This could also be the fastest method. Spindle/Weaving station (I don't know what it's called, but you use it to weave): - Created out of refined wood and sticks. (The ‘needle’ could be created from wood, stone, iron and diamond or gold) gold is just like wood, only more pretty.) - To use it, put wool or cotton in it and it will function like the grindstone (right clicking) can also be combined with a furnace - It will output linen if you put cotton in it. - It will output woven wool if you put wool in it. Wool: - Woven wool (Weaved on a weaving station (What the hell is it called? (Spindle?)) • 1 piece of wool will make 4 woven wool pieces. • If placed on horizontal/vertical surfaces &#61607; It will use 1/8 of a tile but will not be there physically &#61607; It will be white-ish with darker stripes &#61607; When right clicked, it will turn 90 degrees. (Excellent for making patterns.) &#61607; Stacking the wool pieces will simply give you a stack. (where the colours you used match up with the colours in the stack!) • If used for crafting cloths &#61607; The cloths will fit under the armour &#61607; Each part is crafted like armour, but different hats may occur. &#61607; The colour of the clothes will depend on the colour of the woven wool once dye is implemented. &#61607; Patterns can be created by using differently coloured pieces of woven wool. • You will need to make clothing for the arms separately, as the chest piece should only cover the chest (Duh!) &#61607; Could protect from varying temperatures. (Which would be a cool addition to the game) Just some ideas I had for the game. What do you think?
