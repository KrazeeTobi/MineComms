Also, here's an idea to expand the Redstone library of sciency fancy stuff. Introducing the Redstone Beamer block, comprised of a Diamond, a Redstone Torch, and a Gold ingot. What it does is simple; when exposed to a Redstone current or switch/lever/pressure plate, it projects a beam of energy that carries a Redstone current across long distances, greater than 15 blocks at least, which can be received by a Redstone Torch (Redstone Torches supply power by default, and turn off when exposed to a Redstone current, or if someone toggles an adjacent switch, a lever, or a pressure plate). Depending on where it's placed, it will emit the beam in a specific direction. If placed on the side of a block, the beam travels in the direction that the block's side is facing (Beamer on North face of block = Northward beam), whilst placing it on the top or bottom of a block will result in the beam travelling upwards or downwards, respectively. It could come in handy for long-distance contraptions and the like. However, the beam is very much linear, and sometimes you need to redirect it. Enter the Reflector block, essentially a block that acts like a mirror, redirecting the beam in a certain direction. There are two versions of the Reflector; one for reflecting a horizontal beam at a right-angle (reflect it to the left or right of the block), crafted from 3 Cobblestone and 3 Iron ingots in vertical formation, and another for converting a horizontal beam to a vertical beam (reflect it up or down) and vice-versa, requiring the same ingredients in a different configuration (the Iron in a diagonal line, and the Cobblestone filling the bottom corner). The latter version requires different placements to define whether it reflects a beam upwards (place on top of a block) or downwards (place on the underside). Also, right-clicking a Reflector will rotate it according to which side is clicked (clicking on the North side will cause the reflector to face North-West, whilst clicking the West side rotates it to face the South-West), which can allow redirection of the beam. Also, like a normal Redstone current, hitting a TNT block with a Redstone Beam will activate it. Another possible application would involve Torches. Since a Redstone Beam carries a strong current of energy similar to that of an electrical current, having it pass through a naked flame would stimulate stronger ionisation, similar to how microwaves cause a naked flame to give off waves of plasma (remember kids, don't try this at home: watch people doing it on YouTube instead). To this end, the basic idea I have is that if a Redstone Beam passes through a Fire block or a lit Torch (or maybe even a Lantern if Notch ever implements them), the beam would "catch fire" so to speak, and any mob hit by the "Burning Beam" would either take direct damage or burst into flames (whichever seems fairer or fancier), providing a fancy form of trap. Anyways, I know it seems a bit like a laser, but that's pretty much what it is, anyway.
