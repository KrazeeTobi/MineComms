[QUOTE=robmaister12;27950289]If you use relative coordinates (0 - 1 clamped), any texture pack size should work, as long as you define the number of tiles in the texture (0.0625 will always be 1/16th of the texture). At least that's how I got my tilesets working on an Android game I'm developing... Unless minecraft decided to take (16, 16) and convert it into texture vertices... which seems really stupid. I haven't actually looked at any of the decompiled code, so I am currently clueless as to how Notch works with tilesets, I'm just speculating here.[/QUOTE] Thinking about this, I think I worked out how it's done, but I hope I'm wrong (since it's stupid) The only problem with 32x32 packs and such is the fire/water animation being drawn over the normal textures, the actual texture offsets (etc.) themselves scale properly, [b]but[/b] that would mean Notch is doing animation by taking the desired frame, drawing it over the base texture map, and then setting the modified texture to be the texture used to render the scene. That'd lead to a lot of memory churn, doing animation by just changing the frame used (and then the position based on the frame) would have less memory churn, and would most likely fix the problem with texture pack sizes. I hope I'm wrong though and it's actually something else entirely.
