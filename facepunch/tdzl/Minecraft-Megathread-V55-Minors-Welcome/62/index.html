Update on squid farm experiment: Too impractical to build on land. It requires you to eliminate EVERY possible spawning point of normal animals within a huge radius, and on some maps this can prove to be near impossible. On top of THAT you have to eliminate any water spots where you DON'T want squid to pop up in order to increase farm efficiency. Likely solution: Terra-forming the bottom of a lake large enough for one to build a normal mob trap on (over 80 blocks wide). This may prove highly effective as 90% of my squid encounters have been while swimming through a MASSIVE sea, too far from land for animals to spawn. Since squid tend to swim downward, it will require carving out inverted pyramids or funnels of varying dimension (or one great big one if you can manage), and at the bottom(s) you have a 2x2 hole with signs keeping water from flowing in. From here the squid will drop through into a seperate water canal that will carry them into a drowning/collection point (drops them onto dry land in front of the player). Unless under-water currents are created the effectiveness of this trap is questionable as it relies on squid to swim towards the exit hole.
