[QUOTE=Zedicus Mann;27635677]I respect you guys for owning it for so long. I've only owned Minecraft for about a year now.[/QUOTE] For me, it is just over two years at this point. Minecraft is basically what got me into Facepunch. Hell, I'll give you guys a quick history because I'm feeling old. Lets go back to October of 2009, and I was listening to the Casual Collective web radio. Someone said it was boring and they would go back to playing this strange game called "minecraft". I did the same and after finding the site, was amazed and made lots of awesome stuff. I found multiplayer, and was fully addicted at this point, and an active member of the tiny forum, minecraftforum.net. Back then the community was small and fun. I didn't actually buy the game yet, but there was this gifting program going on at this time which was if you bought the game, you could give a free copy to anyone you wanted. This went from about December 25th to 2010. I was searching some multiplayer servers at this time and stumbed across "Heavy Petters", I'm just messing around there, and some guy called "Doomish" was talking about making a Black Mesa replica in minecraft. I'm interested, and join in the fun. We used Freeman's Mind as a guide, and made a ton of it. It eventually got griefed by "sprite_hater", and we were pretty sad. I eventually found out that this server was made for "Facepunch" members, and I checked out the forum and casually joined to comment on the Black Mesa project. I eventually got hooked, and look where I am now. The server owner, Shaedez, on new year's eve, decided to gift me the game. Just about this time, indev came out. That was a ton of fun, so I played that and multiplayer creative mode on and off. I eventually started Minelife, a popular blog about indev mode that was on Notch's blog at one point, as well as Minecast, a popular minecraft podcast that featured Notch on the 5th episode. ([url]http://minecast.zuriki.net/[/url]) Some point in this time, the game exploded and the forums were overrun. I migrated most of my posting over to here, and the rest is history.
