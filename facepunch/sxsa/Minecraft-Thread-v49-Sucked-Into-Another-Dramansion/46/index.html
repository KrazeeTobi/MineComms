The Small Automatic Minecart Collider (SAMC) is a success. It showed considerable effects. [img]http://gyazo.com/7e268eaa24b86331247edf22f13cebf5.png[/img] With the data gathered from this experiment, I deduce that everything in the minecraft world, all matter, is made from tiny entities. These entities, I call "Chez Particles", make up everything. Everything. You, me, that creeper, the smoke from the creeper's explosion. Reactions between these "Chez Particles", or "CPs", make everyday events such as minecart movement and explosions. When these particles move at high speeds, they eventually reach the speed of "render". "Render" is the speed on which things appear to you. Just go to a high mountain looking down, and look across the land. If you were not there, you will see a slight "render lag", where bits of landscape appear to appear. Now when minecarts reach the render max, they move strangely. They start shifting in the y and x axis, moving side to side, not up. This causes them to move to other tracks. We don't know why these CPs move this way, and hopefully will find out how. Now when minecarts are in close proximity to each other, they start to chain together inside each other. A theory is that each the Chez Particles that make up minecarts are attracted to other minecart CPs, and they link together. Why do they go into each other? We don't know, but a popular theory is that there is a lot of empty space inside each CP, and there is plenty of space to compact.
