You have to assume the following: - Any player who passes within render distance of your chests, [B]regardless of actual vision and line-of-sight on your area[/B] is able to see all chests you may have placed. Play accordingly and long for the day when such hacking becomes impossible. :) Edit: This is why distance from spawn is the largest factor in determining "safeness" of an area. Basically, as you travel outwards from spawn, the pure random percent change that a player will stumble onto your base is determined by dividing the render area of any given player by the total area of a ring drawn around spawn with radius as your current distance from spawn and edge width the size of a player's render distance. That is, as you move X blocks from spawn in radial distance, your chance of being found by pure random luck decreases linearly. Example: You are 10,000 blocks from spawn. The "hacker" is on render distance "Far" = 10 chunks in all directions from himself loaded = 160 block radius loaded. 160 x 160 = 25,600 block area loaded at any time. For the hacker to find your base, his loaded area has to align with the total area covered by your base, which for simplicity, we will say is a single block :) So there are 25,600 different X/Z paired blocks that the hacker can be located at and still technically see your base through hacking. Now the total area at your distance is calculated as following: Area = (Spawn_Distance + 160) * (Spawn_Distance + 160) - (Spawn_Distance - 160) * (Spawn_Distance - 160) = 10160*10160 - 9840*9840 = 103225600 - 96825600 = [B]6,400,000[/B] total X/Z coordinate pairs that overlap a block at 10,000 "radius" from spawn (any block at X = 10k or Z = 10k). So by pure random chance, the hacker has a 25600/6400000 = .004 probability = .4% chance to come within range of your base. By the same math, being at 20k, the hacker would have a .2% chance, 100k = .04% chance, 1M = .004% chance, etc. TL;DNR: Felt like doing math. Get away from spawn for safety.
