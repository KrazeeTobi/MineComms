[QUOTE=gman_beeman;35541256]If I put a Nether portal at 0, 0 in the Nether, will I spawn at 0, 0 in the Normal world?[/QUOTE] Late post I guess but no one really answered this question with technical information. Thus, me. You only place a portal in the nether at 0,0 (assuming y is equivalent as well for simplicity), and you [b]don't[/b] place a portal in the overworld at 0,0. Upon taking the nether portal, the game will calculate the resulting coordinates in the overworld: 0*8,0*8 = 0,0. Then it will search within a 33x33x128 block height [b]centered[/b] on that coordinate set for the closest "decent location" from the coordinates (spherically) to generate a nether portal. Now the odds that 0,0 happens to be a perfect spot for a portal are pretty slim. So you end up with a portal which might be as far away as 33 blocks. In the overworld, this isn't a big deal because 33 blocks translating back to the nether coordinates is within 4-5 blocks from your original portal. But when you create a portal in the overworld and go into the nether, you can offset your resultant portal by an equivalent [b]33 * 8 = 264[/b] blocks in the overworld, which is why it can be very common (around spawn anyway) to create a portal, go into the nether, and come back into the overworld through a completely different portal, hundreds of blocks away. [URL="http://www.minecraftwiki.net/wiki/Nether_Portal#Linkage_between_Overworld_and_Nether"]More info.[/URL]
