[highlight]DOOMISH'S GUIDE TO INDEV (infdev works too i guess)[/highlight] [B]PART FOUR: THE FURNACE AND HOW YOU USE IT[/B] [img]http://www.minecraftwiki.net/images/0/0f/Furnace.png[/img] This is the Furnace. [img]http://www.minecraftwiki.net/images/5/5f/Craftingfurnace.png[/img] This is how you craft the Furnace. Before the furnace existed, smelting things was as easy as dropping blocks into fire and out popped a smelted block. Now it takes input and output as well as a separate block. Here is what the smelting menu looks like: [img]http://www.minecraftwiki.net/images/a/a5/Smeltingmenu.png[/img] To make it work in the first place, you need to put something in the bottom left box. This can be any wood item or coal. To be specific, you can put in logs, planks, sticks, and oddly, crafting tables and chests, as well as coal. Each has a specific burning time and the number of things it burns before it is used up. Here is the table: Coal burns for 80 seconds Log burns for 15 seconds Plank burns for 15 seconds Stick burns for 5 seconds It takes ten seconds to smelt one individual object. As you can see, coal is vastly superior. It is recommended not to use Log blocks because they burn for the same amount of time as planks, and there are four plank blocks to be gotten from a log in the first place. The flame icon above the bottom left box indicates how much fuel is left in one unit of your power supply material. When the icon is empty, that unit is gone forever and it vanishes. The arrow in the middle represents how close to being finished one unit of your smelting material is. It refills every time a material is finished. The material you want to smelt goes in the top left box. Here are the items that you can put into a furnace and the result you get out of it: Iron Ore -> Iron Bar Gold Ore -> Gold Bar Sand -> Glass Cobblestone -> Stone Pork -> Grilled Pork Furnaces as blocks take the longest time to break of any block, though the process can be accelerated with a pickaxe. However, as with any block, they can still be destroyed if they are exactly one block's length away from a Creeper when it explodes. A helpful glitch in case you do not have any torches is simple to do. Start smelting something so that your furnace lights up. Then, before the flame icon empties, take everything out of the furnace and break the furnace. Store it in your inventory until next time it is dark or you run out of torches, then place it. It will be lit when you place it and stay that way until you right click on it or break it again. It is useful as a floodlight in that manner.
