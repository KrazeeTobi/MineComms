A post on Reddit inspired me to do my own statistical analysis on Diamond. I've decided to do my own tests. This is a 5382400 block area of terrain generated in an unmodded 1.4.4 client, analyzed with MCedit. World Type: Default Generate Structures: OFF Seed: -5264452076474991435 This first chart shows you the raw number of diamonds per layer. [t]http://i.imgur.com/1mmKv.png[/t] This second chart shows you your average diamonds per layer at every four layers. In other words, if you did a branch mine and dug out layers 8 and 9, you would reveal layer 7 below you and layer 10 above you, hence you can see layers 7, 8, 9, and 10. [t]http://i.imgur.com/v71Ky.png[/t] You get the most raw diamond mining so that layers 5, 6, 7, and 8 are visible. However, you would only see a dramatic difference over an EXTREMELY long period of time, probably months or even years. Personally, I would recommend mining at layers 9, 10, 11, and 12 if you can deal with having to jump over a block of lava every now and then, or layers 10, 11, 12, and 13 if you want the most diamonds whilst remaining relatively safe. Conclusion: Mine somewhere between layers 6 & 11 feet level, you'll generally get the optimum amount of diamonds around here. As to which layers are the best exactly, go with whatever makes you feel most comfortable
