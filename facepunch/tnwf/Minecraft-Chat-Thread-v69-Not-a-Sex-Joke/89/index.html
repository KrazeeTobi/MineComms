[editline]18th December 2012[/editline] I've been talking with Artix about ideas for seasons in the game. Each Minecraft day (one complete day-night cycle from sunrise to sunrise,) is 20 minutes long. Each Minecraft lunar cycle, or month (from full moon to full moon,) lasts 8 Minecraft days, or 160 minutes (or 2 2/3 hours.) We propose that if there are to be seasons, each season could last one Minecraft lunar month. Given that there are presumably 4 seasons, this would mean that one Minecraft "year" would last 640 Minutes (or 10 2/3 hours.) For fun, each new year could be marked by a total solar eclipse, which might result in a day of darkness in which mobs could spawn. (An alternative to a blood moon concept.) Seasons would be purely visible. Water would never freeze nor thaw in biomes, and certain biomes might never be affected at all (such as taigas or deserts.) In other biomes, however, snow might fall without the ice freezing over, or leaves might fall on the ground around trees in a snow-like layer leaving behind the brambles on the trees where leaves once were. [editline]18th December 2012[/editline] For futher fun, seasons might be configurable similar to the flatworld configuration. You could chose to put in speed multipliers per season or generate a world with only one season, so you could make an entirely autumn world or an entirely spring world to make it like original minecraft, or you could chose which season you start out in during the regular cycle.
