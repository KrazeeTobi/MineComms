Classy. On a lighter note, I had a dream last night that seems somewhat related to Minecraft. I was in some rocky deserty place that looked like it was in America or the Mediterranean, and I was digging this large canyon through the mountainous area. Given that this dream was in the usual real-life graphics style, it actually looked pretty awesome, though I had to stop digging once I approached a pool of water that seemed to be connected to a rushing river. I recall two people walking down the canyon path towards the old barely-inhabited "ghost town" that the canyon ended at; I assume they were either mountaineers or just travellers. Strangely, I had no pickaxe; only a shovel, so I can guess all my picks had been worn out, which is worrying since in Minecraft I make sure to repair my Iron Pickaxes when they're almost completely worn out, with only the occasional breakage and "GODDAMNIT!" breaking that particular pattern. Looking back at my work, I saw this strange abandoned building up on one of the mountainous outcrops; it looked like a cross between a church, an old hospital and St Francis' Folly from Tomb Raider. I don't think I went to explore there, since I later found myself in a building that seemed to be a cross between a mall and a community centre; there was even what appeared to be a bar, and my first thoughts were "I'd love to order a Jack Daniels and Coke, but I'm only 20 and this is America". My guess is that the source material for this dream was memories of my job on Thursday clearing out plant-life from this decommissioned railway station at West Grinstead, memories of the Milk Bar from Majora's Mask (I had been watching plenty of ChuggaaConroy's Majora's Mask LP recently), memories of my time in the American Southwest, memories of New Vegas, and of course memories of Minecraft. Just something cool that happened to me last night.
