[QUOTE=MedicWine;38992487]Not sure if this is right thread to ask this (I've been away from facepunch for a while) but whats the best way to power a quarry in FTB? I've been using a steam engine, but I dont want to have to keep feeding it buckets of water.[/QUOTE] You really should have been busy on pumping oil and refining it, because a Combustion Engine from BuildCraft's the best one at (I think) 5MJ/t with fuel, 2MJ/t with oil, and 1MJ/t with lava. As for the buckets of water, depending on the FTB version (MindCrack pack or FTB Beta A), you should be using an Aqueous Accumulator. Presuming you're using FTB Beta A, one Accumulator can keep at least two Steam Engines completely filled. Two Steam Engines at one Aqueous Accumulator makes 4MJ/t. Being one joule off. One Steam Engine can be used to pump oil into whatever. I personally pump it into a Liquid Transposer, where the oil gets canned on the spot for later refining. Oh, right. Quarries. They take a minimum of 2MJ/t for a slow excavation, but full speed occurs at 9MJ/t. That silly Steam Engine's only the bare bone to power it. You can set up multiple engines of sorts to power one, but my best method involves two Combustion Engines with fuel and a Redstone Energy Cell to collect the excess 1MJ/t. It's like a battery of a sort. Placing an Aqueous Accumulator in a small 3x1 or 2x2 pool will be very quick on the filling part. To keep them compact, you can place engines adjacent to each other and turn them with a wrench (BC's. OmniTools will be soon.). Be weary when using fuel. One can is probably more than plentiful for most tasks. Start with one and add accordingly occasionally. Oh, and [highlight]WATCH YOUR TEMPERATURES![/highlight] If they get too hot, they explode like TNT or a Creeper's kamikaze. The following configuration takes a while to heat to the point of total water consumption. Place TWO A. Accumulators per C. Engine. [i]Front: Aqueous Accumulators are placed in a 3x2x1 (per engine) pool of water and are configured to output the water to its top side, where the C. Engines are placed. Placing the A. Accumulators in a pool of water significantly increases its production, like a more advanced pump that only works on water. Being adjacent, there are two separate levers powering the C. Engines. A Redstone Energy Cell is collecting any excess joules. This one's set to 5/0MJ in/output. You don't want it outputting as well.[/i] [t]http://i.imgur.com/lnxME.png[/t] [i]Back: Showing that the Aqueous Accumulators are in a 3x1 pool.[/i] [t]http://i.imgur.com/DP1FR.png[/t] tl;dr: * You should have been pumping oil first to get the most energy. * You should have been refining said oil to fuel to get even more energy. * Invest in a few Liquid Transposers to can oil or fuel on the spot. * Liquid Transposers can also extract oil/fuel to a refinery if configured. * Pay attention to the joule output of all your engines. * Aqueous Accumulators are a life saver and don't cost joules to power. * For some reason, this set-up produces 12MJ/t.
