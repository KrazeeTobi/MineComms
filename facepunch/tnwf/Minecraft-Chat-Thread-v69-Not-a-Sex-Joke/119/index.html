[QUOTE=MeltingData;39407347]How do you even put something like that together?[/QUOTE] Each computer uses 4 colors of the 16 on the bundled cable. So 2 colors for sending and 2 colors for receiving, one of the colors is a trigger so whenever the trigger is activate it tells the computer to look at the accompanying wire that is either on or off at the time, representing a 1 or a 0. Like White/Orange = Signal/Trigger so when Orange is "on", the machine checks and if at that time White isn't on, then it's a 0, if White is on it's a 1. I could have used more colors for each computer and sent more data at once but I thought I wanted to cram in more clients per cable :v: The rest is just processing everything and making sure shit doesn't collide, collisions are the worst shit :suicide:
