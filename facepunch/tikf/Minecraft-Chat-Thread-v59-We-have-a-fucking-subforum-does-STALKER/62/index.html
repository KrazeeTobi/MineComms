Here is my circuit: [img_thumb]http://k.min.us/ijCKvE.png[/img_thumb] I want it to incessantly play notes.[/QUOTE] To make a repeating loop you have to do something like this: Have everything connected until you give it power. [IMG]http://i8.photobucket.com/albums/a7/IceWarrior98/Screenshots/Minecraft/2011-02-25_205529.png[/IMG] As soon as you give it power, disconnect the power source immediately. [IMG]http://i8.photobucket.com/albums/a7/IceWarrior98/Screenshots/Minecraft/2011-02-25_205519.png[/IMG] You now have a continuous loop. I use a similar design for my lighthouse flashes. the only difference is I use a lever. Levers work better because you dont have to break the circuit at all. For some reason however, pressure plates send a solid signal if left alone. [IMG]http://i8.photobucket.com/albums/a7/IceWarrior98/Screenshots/Minecraft/2011-02-25_210131.png[/IMG] The trick is to use the delay to your advantage. If the delay is long enough for the source to be turned off, it will continuously send the signal throughout the circuit. Also, repeaters are diodes. They only send the signal one way. Anything else is ignored. An example of flashing redstone lights (lighthouse simulation) [IMG]http://i8.photobucket.com/albums/a7/IceWarrior98/Screenshots/Minecraft/2011-02-25_210534.png[/IMG]
