Out of boredom, I decided to figure this out. I timed it, and the minecraft character walks 64 blocks in 15 seconds on perfectly flat ground. That's 256 blocks a minute and 15,360 blocks an hour. Earlier somebody posted that maps extend 32 million blocks in each direction from where you spawn. Hypothetically, though, because somebody tried it and went to 2 or 3 million before the game had rounding errors and flipped out. So, hypothetically, it would take you 2083.3 hours to get to the end of the earth. That's 86.8 days. And this is only true if the earth is perfectly flat and you walk in a straight line without stopping. But in reality, with the 2-3 million block limit, it would take you 130-195 hours. That's 5.4-8.1 days. Correct me if I'm wrong. But that's crazy. Horizontal limits aren't a problem I think.
I think you just spent time calculating something that most people won't bother actually trying. It pretty much ruins your save if you go that far.
Oh and just for kicks, walking from one end of the earth under the same conditions to the other end would take you 173.6 days. Or a little over 5 months.
