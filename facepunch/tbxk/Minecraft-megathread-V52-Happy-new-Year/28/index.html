Here's an idea to truly open up a whole new "world" for Minecraft. The Nether was an interesting experiment, but it's simply a place to die, for what it's worth (giant flying jellyfish ghosts that spit fire, a glowing dust that doesn't really do anything as of yet, rock that never stops burning, red mushrooms, etc). I come here today with an idea for a brand new "Realm", a place quite unlike the Nether, but with similar standards of peril. Ladies and gentlemen, I give to you [B]The Sky[/B], a high-flying overworld populated with mysterious floating Temples left behind by an ancient culture. It's an instance-like place, similar to the Nether, in the fact that you have to activate a Temple Gate (like the Portal for Nether access) in order to enter. To construct a Temple Gate, first create a frame of Gold blocks (that's right, blocks of Gold), three blocks wide by four blocks tall, with an empty space of 2 blocks in the centre. Then, place a Wooden Door in the empty space. Ensure it remains closed for the next part. Finally, place a Sign on the front of the top block, and type, in this exact way, By The Sky ------ Let The Way Be Opened Once the Gate is made, simply open the door, and a golden glow will appear in the frame. Stand in the glow as you would a Nether Portal, and you will be whisked away to a Temple in the Sky, where a new adventure awaits you every time. If this doesn't happen, you probably did something wrong Concerning the sign one places on the Temple Gate, the space with the hyphens can be left blank, or you can type in a specific six-character code to determine which Temple the gate leads to. Each randomly-generated Temple has it's own randomly-generated code, and every time you use the Gate without a code, you are sent to a random Temple, so remember to look at the sign of the Temple Gate on the other side, to memorise the code and be able to return to that Temple later. When in the Sky, the first thing one notices is that they always enter inside a Temple, as the Temple Gates there serve as bridges between the surface world and the Temple-littered Sky. The blocks that comprise the body of the Temple, and the Sky-side Temple Gate, are indestructible, so don't try breaking them. Should one be able to look down, they will see the surface of the world beneath the high-flying clouds. Another thing to mention is that the Sky has the same day-night cycle as the surface, so if you go at sunrise, it'll be sunrise in the Sky, and it'll take the same time to reach sunset as it would on the surface. Needless to say, the Sky isn't really a place for mining, but it is certainly a place for adventuring, with each Temple having many rooms, many traps, many enemies, and many many treasures. Consider it to be Adventure Mode, if you will. Many treasures can be found in the Temples, including Ether Shards, a unique material used in the crafting of Tomes, which brings me to the next subject. A week or two ago, I remember someone saying something about a magic system, and I said it'd only fit into Minecraft if it were component-based, what with the game being oriented around crafting and all. Well, I think i've cracked it with this system. Firstly, you'd need to craft an Inert Tome, comprised of an Ether Shard (top), a Book (centre), two pieces of Leather (either side), and a Gold Bar (bottom). Then, you'd need to craft it with a specific reagent, turning it into a tool that actually does something. Once crafted with a reagent, you will need to have enough of that reagent in your inventory in order for it to work. For example, a Feather on top of the Tome will yield a Tome of Lightness, which when used grants 5 seconds of lowered gravity, decreasing fall speed and whatnot, at the cost of 1 Feather. A fair price for being able to gently float like Chickens do. Other examples include using Leather in a Protection spell, Snowballs in an Ice spell, Sulphur in a Blast spell, and so on. However, the number of times you can use a Tome isn't just limited by the quantity of the reagent, but also the quality of the Ether Shard used in the crafting. A Small Ether Shard (most common, practically GROWS in some areas) yields a Lesser Tome, which can only cast 25 instances of the spell before reverting to a regular Book, whilst a regular Shard (common in Temple Chests) gives a regular Tome, capable of casting 50. Large Shards (slightly rarer, only spawns in one Chest per Temple) yield 100-use Greater Tomes, whilst the ultra-rare Massive Shards (has a small chance to spawn in a Temple Chest) yields Supreme Tomes, capable of 200 uses. Crafting 4 Shards of the same type will yield a higher grade, but you will require 9 Large Shards to craft a Massive Shard. So combining Shards isn't technically practical in the long run, although since Small Shards are so common and can actually be grown when in the right conditions, you might end up with more Small Shards than you know what to do with, and thus you might be able to afford Shard-crafting. So, to wrap up my idea; new Sky world with floating Temples, can be reached by making a Temple Gate, Temples are Adventure Mode, can find Ether Shards to make magic books with, component-based magic system, Tomes have limited uses, Shards can be crafted together into better Shards. Hope it's awesome, hope it's cool, hope it's viable. Speak if you can see any faults, point them out, keep it constructive. Adieu.
