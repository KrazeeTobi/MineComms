[U] Minecraft? - WAHT IS TAHT?[/u] [Quote=Notch]Minecraft is a game about placing blocks to build anything you can imagine. At night monsters come out, make sure to build a shelter before that happens. It also has music by C418! [/quote] Minecraft is an indie (short for Independant) game, developed by Markus Persson, A.K.A. Notch. It's been under development since about May, 2009. In the game, you place, and destroy blocks in a world generated completely by random. World generation is amazing, as it can create worlds that are [B]over 5 times the size of the Earth![/B] That's insanely huge. Anyway, you can use these blocks to create your house, or just dig a hole and wait for night to pass. [U] Blocks [/u] [img]http://www.minecraftwiki.net/images/3/32/Template1.png[/img] There are many, many blocks in Minecraft, so let's just go over the basics, shall we? You can find a complete list [url=http://www.minecraftwiki.net/wiki/Blocks] Here[/url]. [B] DIRT [/B] [IMG]http://www.minecraftwiki.net/images/0/06/Dirt2.png[/IMG] Dirt is one of the first blocks introduced in the game. Dirt is simple to mine and is often acquired in large amounts when unmonitered during its excavation. It will always drop a resource block, regardless of how it was mined. It may be mined by hand fairly quickly, but using a shovel will significantly speed up the process. Using a hoe on a placed block of dirt will prepare the block for use in farming. After using a hoe on a block of dirt, the top of the dirt's appearance changes, becoming ridged and bumpy like tilled soil. When a block of tilled dirt is introduced to a block of water, it becomes darker to replicate the appearance of wet soil. Dirt will also change into Grass, if it is exposed to sunlight, and next to a grass block. [B] TREES [/B] [img]http://www.minecraftwiki.net/images/7/7e/Tree1.png[/img] Trees are composed of wood and leaf blocks. Tree harvesting is an essential first step for any player in Survival mode. Trunks and branches can be harvested for wood without any tools; one wood block may be crafted into four wooden planks, from which tools can be made. Wood can be harvested more quickly using an axe. [b] COAL [/B] [img]http://www.minecraftwiki.net/images/f/fb/Coalore.png[/img] Coal Ore is a mineral that is a common sight on almost all maps. It is found at any elevation, inside caverns, cliff faces, and underground. A lump of coal can be mined directly from Coal ore using a wooden pickaxe or better. Coal lumps can be combined with sticks to make torches, or used as fuel in a furnace or Minecart. When burned in a furnace, it provides 8 uses for each coal "lump". [B] IRON [/B] [img]http://www.minecraftwiki.net/images/8/87/Ironore2.png[/img] In the game, iron ore can be smelted in a furnace to make an iron ingot; iron ingots are used to make all the medium quality tools: armor and Flint and Steel. Iron ore requires a stone, iron, or diamond pickaxe to successfully extract. [B] GOLD [/B] [img]http://www.minecraftwiki.net/images/1/19/Goldore.png[/img] Gold, despite what you may thing, is actually a terrible material. With the durability of wood, it is more used as decoration, rather than tools. It can also be used to craft a watch. [B] DIAMOND [/B] [img] http://www.minecraftwiki.net/images/d/db/Diamondore2.png[/img] Diamond ore is generally considered to be the most valuable and elusive block in the game. It can be found generally in a vein of 4 blocks, and you may often find lava in its proximity and vice versa. However, it can only be mined with an Iron pickaxe or better. The diamond gems can be used right away in a workbench to craft diamond tools, weapons and armor, which are currently the most durable in the game. ou can also create diamond blocks by crafting nine diamond gems together in a 3x3 square using the workbench. Stay away from them, or use a bow! Skeletons fire arrows at you. Strafe them and hit them with your sword! Spiders are dangerous due to their jumping range and long hit range. They're harmless in daytime, though. Don't hit them! Zombies are slow, albeit dangerous. Kill them easily by hitting them, going back, and repeating. Ghasts are the fuck your shit up kind of things. They float and fire fireballs at you, kill them by hitting the balls back at them. Now, Minecraft is in beta. Which means, we've got mor- less bugs, and much, much more fun.
