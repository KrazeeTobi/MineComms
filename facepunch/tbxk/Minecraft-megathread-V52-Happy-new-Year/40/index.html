I suppose my main problem with survival is that it isn't really survival. The AI can be far too easily tricked, it simply results in following set patterns. Death is usually a complete accident and comes out of nowhere. For it to be truly survival, I feel it should pressure the player more continuously. The AI should be causing block damage e.g. zombies battering on doors until you stick a sword through the window, creepers exploding if you get too close to them, even when there's a glass window in the way (i.e. the enemy should be able to see through partially transparent blocks); or other intelligent AI actions e.g. spiders climbing walls, spider-riding skeletons doing as the Mongols i.e. kiting the player. The Ghasts are fine imo, they're quite difficult to kill if you're not really good with the bow, although they need to be intelligent enough to avoid lava falls, which i've noticed to be their #1 cause of death. Food should become a necessity. Perhaps not water, that'd just be the same as food requirement i.e. you're essentially doing the same thing twice. Sleep would be annoying, and would remove any possibility of a tense night, dimming the lights so the monsters don't come to peek through the window; it's just be "press x to sleep" and then morning appears. Good ideas, or should I wear a dunce cap for the rest of the day?
