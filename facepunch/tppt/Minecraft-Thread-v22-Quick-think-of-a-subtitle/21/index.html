[QUOTE=LCBADs;23120253]Yes please.[/QUOTE] Word. It's gonna need to wait until tomorrow or Tuesday if you want complex shit or a video, because I just realized I have to work tomorrow and I'm kinda blitzed. But I'll toss up the basic set quick. First, read this: [url]http://www.ee.surrey.ac.uk/Projects/Labview/gatesfunc/index.html[/url] Makes your life a whole lot easier if you know those symbols, because the complex stuff is mostly just variations on the basic gates strung together. Also, I should probably stop using the term "switch" for all my inputs, but right now I'm using only switches for inputs. Obviously everything from buttons to pressure pads work for this. Anyway before you get started- you know that a torch on a block acts as an inverter, right? I kinda assume everyone knows at least that. That's also a basic logic gate, a NOT gate, which does pretty much what it sounds like it should. So, okay, basic logic gates. OR: [URL=http://s184.photobucket.com/albums/x281/Xenocidebot/Minecraft/Minecraft%20logic/?action=view&current=orgate.png][IMG]http://i184.photobucket.com/albums/x281/Xenocidebot/Minecraft/Minecraft%20logic/th_orgate.png[/IMG][/URL] This one's the simplest. If either of those switches are flipped, or both are flipped, you get an output. It's also fucking useless. NOR: [URL=http://s184.photobucket.com/albums/x281/Xenocidebot/Minecraft/Minecraft%20logic/?action=view&current=norgate.png][IMG]http://i184.photobucket.com/albums/x281/Xenocidebot/Minecraft/Minecraft%20logic/th_norgate.png[/IMG][/URL] Same as an OR, but with an inverter on it. So, now, if either switch is flipped, or both are, you get no output. AND: [URL=http://s184.photobucket.com/albums/x281/Xenocidebot/Minecraft/Minecraft%20logic/?action=view&current=andgate.png][IMG]http://i184.photobucket.com/albums/x281/Xenocidebot/Minecraft/Minecraft%20logic/th_andgate.png[/IMG][/URL] I put the one torch on the top, but it can be on the side. This one's pretty simple too- you need both switches flipped to get an output. One switch on or both off, and nothing. NAND: [URL=http://s184.photobucket.com/albums/x281/Xenocidebot/Minecraft/Minecraft%20logic/?action=view&current=notandgate.png][IMG]http://i184.photobucket.com/albums/x281/Xenocidebot/Minecraft/Minecraft%20logic/th_notandgate.png[/IMG][/URL] If you couldn't guess, it's just an AND gate with an inverter stuck on it. As long as one of your inputs is off, you've got output. If both are on, you've got no output. XOR (EXOR): [URL=http://s184.photobucket.com/albums/x281/Xenocidebot/Minecraft/Minecraft%20logic/?action=view&current=xorgate.png][IMG]http://i184.photobucket.com/albums/x281/Xenocidebot/Minecraft/Minecraft%20logic/th_xorgate.png[/IMG][/URL] Yeah, I shamelessly copied Wev's, I can't make it any smaller. Anyway this one's a set of NORs strung together. It gives you an output if one input is on. Both on or off, nothing. XNOR (EXNOR): By now you shouldn't need a picture to know that this is just an XOR gate with an inverter on it, and that it behaves accordingly. NOT A GATE (JUST A TIMER): [URL=http://s184.photobucket.com/albums/x281/Xenocidebot/Minecraft/Minecraft%20logic/?action=view&current=clock.png][IMG]http://i184.photobucket.com/albums/x281/Xenocidebot/Minecraft/Minecraft%20logic/th_clock.png[/IMG][/URL] Inverters have a delay. An odd number of them, wired together thus, should regularly pulse on and off. Just make sure you don't use too few, or they'll burn out. Works good as a clock for other gates. LATCH: [URL=http://s184.photobucket.com/albums/x281/Xenocidebot/Minecraft/Minecraft%20logic/?action=view&current=rsflipfloplatch.png][IMG]http://i184.photobucket.com/albums/x281/Xenocidebot/Minecraft/Minecraft%20logic/th_rsflipfloplatch.png[/IMG][/URL] [URL=http://s184.photobucket.com/albums/x281/Xenocidebot/Minecraft/Minecraft%20logic/?action=view&current=rsflipfloplatchunderside.png][IMG]http://i184.photobucket.com/albums/x281/Xenocidebot/Minecraft/Minecraft%20logic/th_rsflipfloplatchunderside.png[/IMG][/URL] Just a pair of NOR gates wired together. Bistable circuit, so it can hold information. Or, you know, be stable in one of two states. WOOHOO! You're gonna be making a calculator before you know it. Just make sure to use buttons or pressure pads for inputs, if you're using switches and both of them are on it will summon the great god Boolio to eat your face. This is where knowing the logic gate symbols is useful- if you knew what a NOR was, you could look at this page: [url]http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Latch_%28electronics%29[/url] and use the RS example to make one out of whatever NOR designs you were using. There are more compact ways of doing it, this is just easier to show you how it's wired. And, hopefully, you could use examples on the web to plan how to wire the more complex stuff yourself.
