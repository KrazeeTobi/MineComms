[QUOTE=Brt5470;32449322]The side effect is sort of like MipMapping. it's a shame MC doesn't do it by default.[/QUOTE] Mip-Mapping makes the textures look pretty bad though at even a short distance, there's not much detail you get can out of 8x8 or 4x4 (and it "smears" alpha, so things like ladders become solid after a short distance)
[QUOTE=TheDecryptor;32450924]Mip-Mapping makes the textures look pretty bad though at even a short distance, there's not much detail you get can out of 8x8 or 4x4 (and it "smears" alpha, so things like ladders become solid after a short distance)[/QUOTE] That's because you need anisotropic filtering. However, forced aniso and mipmapping don't work well together (you get those outlines), so they'd need to be added in a mod (OptiFine has started doing this, but the AA method is very slow, so it's not really worth it, it's better to just force aniso on its own).
[QUOTE=TheDecryptor;32450924]Mip-Mapping makes the textures look pretty bad though at even a short distance, there's not much detail you get can out of 8x8 or 4x4 (and it "smears" alpha, so things like ladders become solid after a short distance)[/QUOTE] That's not a fault of mipmapping in general, nearly all games use it, or else everyone would have to run AA to stop texture flicker.
Most games use textures larger than 16x16 though, so a drop from 1024 to 512 isn't noticeable.
