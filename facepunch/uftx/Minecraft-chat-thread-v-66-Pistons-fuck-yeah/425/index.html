[editline]3rd December 2011[/editline] comma[/QUOTE] I don't think you're understanding what I'm trying to say (and I admit I'm probably doing a poor job of explaining it too). When you start a world with a seed, the terrain generator uses that seed to extrapolate information for building terrain. It processes the seed using some kind of algorithm; the terrain generator is the same in everyone's Minecraft and it provides the same output when you give it the same input. That's why we can share seeds in the first place, because the terrain gen interprets the information in the same way across every version of Minecraft. Villages are generated as part of the terrain. Hence, if you give someone a seed with a village near spawn (assuming the spawn doesn't "wander" like it did at one point), when they create a world with that seed, the village will be near spawn. NOW. Assuming that the big villages mod does NOT alter village placement/generation but instead takes the villages [b]inherent in the seed[/b] and builds more stuff around them, if someone had the mod and took the seed that RaptorRed provides, they would get the same result, which is that colossal village that we saw. I don't mean to sound condescending, and I could very well be wrong, but as far as I can tell people weren't understanding what I was trying to convey.
