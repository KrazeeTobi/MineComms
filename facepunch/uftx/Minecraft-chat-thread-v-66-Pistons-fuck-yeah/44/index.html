[QUOTE=DrasarSalman;31327969]It doesn't mean you need to buy a new computer. If you just have an old desktop, you may also have a screen, a keyboard and a mouse. Some components may be worth changing, but we aren't talking about Crysis specs. Your network can also be on just 300 Mbps instead of 1000. What I was thinking is that this is easier than calling some small company if your server are having problems.[/QUOTE] Having a gigabit connection isn't going to make your internet connection any faster, based on the average connection speeds around the world. Minecraft isn't especially bandwidth intensive. I monitored b/w usage when 20 people were connected to a server, and upstream capped at around 250 kb/s. Additionally, having a "modern graphics card" won't make a difference at all. Here are what specs are taken into account: disk speed (SSD's are optimal), processor speed (chunk generation, keeping track of player actions), and ram allocation (more is better, you'll need it for plugins and additional players). I was able to run a 10-person minecraft server temporarily off of a SSD-based netbook on a 2 megabit up connection. It worked fine, and no one complained of lag. Eventually I switched to Brohoster (this was in October of last year IIRC), but the SSD netbook setup worked great as a temporary solution when I was just learning about hosting Minecraft servers.
