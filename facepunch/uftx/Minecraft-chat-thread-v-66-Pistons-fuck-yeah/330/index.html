I wrote a little Minecraft story, from the point of view of a really imaginative first time player I guess. [QUOTE] Pure, and stunning white. It was all that stretched in every direction for miles; a pure, piercingly bright white sand. I awoke with a ringing in my ears, and the sun fixed high in the sky. There was nothing I could do but move, anywhere away from the desert. But I could not find a path. No definite exit from the dry pit I had found myself in. I wandered for what must have been days, when finally, on the horizon; the most beautiful landscape imaginable. Impossibly massive mountains, with shear cliff faces, probed the sky with tree topped fingers. Caves wove in and out of the cliff sides, joining in the depths of stone. As I moved closer, I found a marsh surrounding the mountains, a marsh created from massive fungal structures; giant mushrooms, plaguing the land and it's surroundings. With awe, I climbed the side of the mountain. As my hand reached for the top, an unfamiliar sound whizzed past my head; an arrow stuck into the ground beside me. Pumping with fear, I turned to find a living skeleton standing in a small cave entrance, it's empty eyes piercing through the darkness. I had no chance of survival, other then to run. As I stepped over the hill, my emotions collided; this immense and beautiful view, to be seen in such a moment of danger. But, with my pause, came great confusion; where were the arrows? And... where was this light coming from? The boney figure had given up it's chase, and taken up a new hobby; spontaneous combustion. As I watched the bones turn to ash, the sun began to set. I moved to the edge of the cliff, and took a seat. Closing my eyes, I allowed the sweet, warm sunlight to penetrate my being, and for once, I was at peace. That peace was cut short, as this was the first time I heard the hiss.[/QUOTE]
