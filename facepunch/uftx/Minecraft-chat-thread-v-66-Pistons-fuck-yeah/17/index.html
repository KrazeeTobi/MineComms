OK, so I decided to play some Minecraft on my serious-play world for once, instead of messing around with Redstone and TooManyItems in a flatgrass map. I hadn't played this world in ages due to, well, laziness, really. So, I went around looking for caves to get stuff from, and found a cave from the surface that was rather vertical. After remembering I needed torches (durr) and going back to get them from my house nearby, I went though it, found a couple iron. (Iron is the best thing I'd found really, other than 3 gold but gold isn't that useful anyway. For reference, I had slightly over a full stack of iron and 3 gold. That's all I had when it comes to ores.) Then, I saved, quitted and went to the Minecraft Wiki for some mining methods. I noticed something about drifting, which is basically finding a cave, getting what you can from it then digging through one of the walls in hopes of finding anohter nearby cave. Since I heard some skeletons at the end of the cave I found earlier, I decided to do that. I did find another cave. But it was bigger than I expected. It was huge and had numerous lava and water streams and falls. I found a bunch of stuff, including my first redstone in that world. After exiting and re-entering due to my pickaxe breaking and me not having any sticks to make a stone pickaxe or something, I found some more stuff. Overall, it had loads of stuff. I gained just under another full stack of iron, about 18 gold and 29 redstone dust. Hell yeah.
