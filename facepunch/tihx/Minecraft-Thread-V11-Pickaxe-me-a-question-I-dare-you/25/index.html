To anyone who thinks minecraft is a resource hog: trust me, it's not. For a 16x16x16 chunk of blocks, it has to take into account 4096 different blocks. That means every time that you go into infdev and take a look out over a landscape on a mountain, you're probably rendering tens of thousands of blocks, and processing hundreds of thousands that are unseen, underground, every frame. And yet, the majority of people here still get a framerate much higher than anybody would ever need.
