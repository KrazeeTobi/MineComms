[b]What exactly is Survival Mode?[/b] Survival Mode was the second gamemode Notch (aka Markus Persson, the creator of Minecraft) has ever made to Minecraft. The gamemode is all what you can imagine by the title - surviving. With the ability to create any structure with a large variety of interesting craftable and obtainable items, endless hours of gameplay. And with the exciting Multiplayer, it just keeps getting better. Survival Mode all started out with Survival Test, with Notch just toying with things that would soon become the incredible gamemode we all play today. Next came /indev/, also known as In Development(?), which was just Survival Test, only, well, I'm not exactly sure why he did this, but he did it for a very good reason I bet. After that came /infdev/, also known as Infinite Development(?), but again, like /indev/. I'm still boggled about why he did this. And finally, after everything was prepared, he released the fantastic game of Minecraft Alpha (also known as Minecraft Survival), the game we HAD until December 20, when the game was updated to Beta and the price was doubled! Further updates will continue to be free but this means the game will be nearing completion far in the future![/release] [release][b]What are mobs?[/b] Mobs are creatures that roam the generated terrain of every level. They can range from nice pigs to green dicks that blow up your shit. There are currently 11 mobs in Survival Mode, with 4 of them being friendly. [b]Pigs[/b] - Pigs are friendly mobs. They may seem cute, but not everyone lives forever. So you'll have to kill such adorable creatures for their delicious bacon that heals 1.5 hearts (cooked bacon heals 4 hearts). [b]Sheep[/b] - Sheep are just as adorable as pigs. You are able magically shear them with your hands by punching them, which results in 1-3 blocks of wool being dropped. [b]Cows[/b] - Cows seem friendly, but they look pretty stupid. But these mobs drop up to 1-3 leather, which can collected to be crafted into really crappy armor. [b]Chickens[/b] - Chickens may look like ducks, but try not to get them confused too much. Chickens can drop from 1-3 feathers when killed. [b]Zombies[/b] - Zombies looks like they're ready to hug, right? Well try not to get too close, because these fuckers pack a punch. They drop 1-3 feathers for God knows why when killed. When it turns day, they begin to burn (there must be no blocks above them, even leaves). [b]Skeletons[/b] - These assholes like to shoot arrows at you from anywhere when they're close to you. Do NOT get trapped in a pit with Skeletons above you. When killed, they drop 1-3 arrows. Just like Zombies, they burn as well when it's day. [b]Spiders[/b] - Spiders lurk anywhere, and are ready to jump at you whenever. They can be a big pain in the ass, especially when there are more than one. When killed, they drop 1-3 string. But with Spiders, they do not start on fire, but most of the time they become friendly, making nice house guests. [b]Slimes[/b] - Slimes are currently broken for some odd reason. But they are very adorable mobs, even if they are unfriendly. Slimes can range from many sizes; smallest slimes don't hurt you, so you can have them follow you and keep them as a pet! When they are killed, they usually drop 1-3(?) slime balls. [b]Creeperssssssssssssp[/b] - These gigantic faggot dicks are the epiphany of why I hate this game at times. But they drop 1-3 gunpowder when they are killed (not blown up) that can be crafted with sand to create TNT. [b]Pig Zombies[/b] - Pig Zombies were added in during the Halloween Update. These large, white, box-like, annoying fuckers shoot fireballs at you from wherever they are floating, even if you aren't that close.
