I remember when the name "Minecraft" meant absolutely nothing to me. Over the summer, I was logged onto Steam, browsing Facepunch. I get a Steam message from a good friend of mine, "Want to watch me Livestream Minecraft?" I responded with, "What the hell is Minecraft?" And so my adventure began. I watched him Livestream for about a half hour, with my occasional comment and question. "The graphics are strange, unique. I like it." I had said. He got killed by a skeleton, and after that, he told me how surprisingly creep(er)y caves in the game were, and I laughed at him. "Scared of cubes? What is so scary about a bunch of squares?" He sent me his account details (he's pretty awesome, by the way) and let me play by mooching off his account. After about a month of non-stop play, I realized...Minecraft. Awesome. About a week after my realization, I brought my laptop to my friends house. After much (and I cannot stress how much I mean by [I]much[/I]) coaxing, I finally got him to log on (using my other friends account details). He was enraptured by the beauty of Minecraft. Now, hear me out. The friend that I referred to was usually on Steam all day erry day. I introduced him to Minecraft. He wasn't on Steam for 2 and a half weeks. When he finally logged on one day, I questioned, "Where the hell were you?" And his response? "Minecraft." Eventually, he bought his own account. Just recently, (about a day or 2 ago, actually) I finally got some money and bought my own account. So concludes my story of Minecraft: A Story of Me.
