I'm actually surprised that people bitch about the length of time between updates. When an update is released, a few things happen: 1) Mods stop working. It generally takes a week or so depending on how motivated the modder/community is to fix the mod so it works again. If updates were released every week, this would become frustrating for the mod developers and in fact many have stopped making SSP mods altogether (the guy who makes Minecart Mania comes to mind). 2) Servers that rely on Bukkit and even HMod don't work anymore. Any server worth going to will be unplayable until an update is released, which as mentioned could take a week or more depending on motivation and commitment. 3) Bugs are introduced. 'Nuff said. Personally I could give two shits about them taking 2-4 months to release an update so long as: a) New content is released in one larger update rather than a few smallish updates. This gives players alot of new stuff to play with rather than just giving them one new toy that they'll get tired of in a day or two because it's the only thing that's new. And, of course, ample time is spent making sure the new content isn't bugged to hell and gone. (leaf decay lag, anyone?) b) Mojang gives a small heads-up to mod developers such as the Bukkit team so that they can quickly get things back up and running. This would mean less server downtime and more happy customers.
