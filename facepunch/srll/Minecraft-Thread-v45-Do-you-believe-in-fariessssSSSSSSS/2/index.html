Short story time :buddy: [quote]As I emerged from my cave, I was amazed to see sunlight. "Is it really morning already?" I said to myself, "My watch must be broken." After a long session of planning, I decided to walk north, where I could see snowy mountains. As I wandered closer, something dawned on me. I was just walking along side a tropical coast. How could there be mountains rivaling Washington here? I was about to turn back and head home, thinking this was all a dream, when suddenly something hit me. "Shit" I thought, "Night already." The object that hit me was an arrow. It could have caused a serious puncture wound, but luckily, my hardened leather vest seemed to reduce all the damage to a mere scrape. This vest would save my life later on. I recovered from the blow, and charged at the beast, only describable as what most folk call a "Evil Spirit" or "Possessed corpse". I just called them "Skeletons". I smacked it's arm with my wooden sword, doing not much other than splintering the blade. I cursed to myself, and began to run. With arrows whizzing by me by the second, I had to hide. I found a small cave, just close enough to slip into without the Skeleton noticing. As I slipped in, I gave a sigh of relief. But the instant I looked forward, I saw a strange shape in the corner. Then I heard that noise... the noise which had ended adventurers many generations before me. "SSSSsssSSSsssssSSSSss....." As I closed my eyes, awaiting the sweet feeling of death, I heard a noise. It was a soft twing, much like rock striking bone. As I opened my eyes to see what happened, the corpse of this "Creeper" slumped onto my shoulders, arrow in head. "Aw hell. The Skeleton." It must have heard the noises from the cave. I was cornered now, all that I had one me were some sand and a couple ounces of gunpowder. "Wait a minute... sand... gunpowder?" After a long bout of thought and dodging arrows, I stopped for a second. That's when it hit me... An arrow. Straight through my now vulnerable left arm. With only seconds of life left, I constructed a small box of explosives. I didn't have enough time to place any sort of fuse, so I tossed it right next to the Skeleton. Thankfully, the impact against the flint inside the gravel walls light the explosives. 2 seconds went by, me and the Skeleton, face to face, motionless. Then, an explosion. Bright light filled the room, and I was sure that I was going to die. But something strange happened. When the explosive went off, it destroyed the caves foundation, and right above the cave was a sheer wall of sand. The sand collapsed, not an inch from my face. I was saved. But trapped. This is where I am now, documenting all that's happened so far. Oddly enough, I can't help but notice these screams coming not more than 500 feet behind me, through what I assume to be a stone wall. It's like the cry of a thousand anguished souls. But that will be a matter to investigate another time. First I must establish a new home, maybe a castle in the mountains. I almost forgot. Climate change like this CANT be natural. Someone must have caused it. Maybe they could tell me more about this region. I guess we'll have to see. -NoPartyHats Day 1 - End [/quote]
