[QUOTE=Dan*;25861454][url]http://crafthub.net/2010/11/04/minecraft-planet/[/url][/QUOTE] :buddy:
Journal Day 1: It has been just over one week since I found those runes in the depths of that desert tomb. The sand fell away beneath me as I crossed through that unusual stone archway. I found myself in absolute darkness. Lighting a torch, it was then I knew I had stumbled into some structure from eons before. Smooth columns rose up around me. Looking in all directions, I could see only the columns closest illuminated by my poultry light source, the naked walls on two sides, and an abyss behind and ahead. Fearing I may get lost in for all I knew was a monolithic structure, I began walking in an arbitrary direction, leaving a trail of redstone dust as I trudged past the seemingly endless stream of columns. No roof was visible, the pillars stretched out of the reach of my pitiful torch. I'm unsure of how long I walked that corridor of stone. I took one break to force some pork down my gullet before continuing on. I knew I had to eat, but that place sapped all feeling of hunger, it made me sick with fear. It was not the darkness or isolation that made me look back every 10 paces, or jump if something unexpected rattled in my backpack. No, it was the silence. Absolute stillness. Whenever I paused for a few seconds when I was sure something walked behind me, not a single note was heard. No wind, no creaking of stone, no noises of the beasts or monsters of above. Just my breath, my footsteps. It was when I realised I only had 3 pieces of pork left and began to panic, that the gigantic hallway came to an abrupt end. It was here I found those runes. A large tablet sat at the end of the hallway, with staircases flanking each side, leading up. Upon this tablet was a language with text I had never before encountered, even in the great Library of Treim. The crude engravings upon this tablet starkly contrasted the sturdy and smooth pillars that rose up so high in the corridor beforehand. It was then I realised that the area around the tablet was not as well chiselled as the rest of this structure. Jagged rocks stuck out at all angles, as if it had just been roughly strip mined and then left. The tablet lay against this jagged wall, it's base still encased within the floor. Several lines of this alien text were scrawled down this obelisk, as well as a diagram near the bottom. The runes near the base displayed what appeared to be a crude depiction of a man, standing by what seemed to be a large doorway. The doorway was split into 14 chunks, with a fire raging on the centre. The following runes showed the man approaching the doorway, and then seemingly disappearing through. The final 2 runes occur at the very bottom of the tablet before it enters the rock. The structure from the previous runes is cut into one side of the stone face, with another doorway cut into the other side, with the man exiting it. In my panic and eagerness to climb the stairs to look for an escape from this stone vault, I gave the runes no thought. I quickly traced the diagrams found on the tablet with some coal and a few sheets of papyrus. It was in a frenzied rush I stumbled up those ancient stairs. Hacking away at dirt above after several minutes of climbing, my spade breached the chasm, and sunlight poured onto my face. I scrambled outside and headed towards the nearest to hill to attempt to get my bearings.
